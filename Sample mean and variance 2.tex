Estimators for $\mu$ and $\sigma^2$ (The sample mean and variance):
\begin{center}
$\hat{\mu} = \bar{X} = \frac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} X_i$\\
$\hat{\sigma}^2=S^2=\frac{1}{n-1}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} (X_i-\bar{X})^2$
\end{center}
First suppose that we have an IID (Independent and identically distributed) sample $X_1,....,X_n$ from a population with mean $\mu$ and variance $\sigma^2$
\begin{enumerate}
\item Showing that the Sample mean is an unbiased estimator of the population mean:\\
\begin{align*}
\mathbb{E}[\bar{X}] 
&=
\mathbb{E}\left[\frac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} X_i\right] \\
&=
\frac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}\mathbb{E}[X_i] \\
&=
\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\mu \\
&= 
\frac{n\mu}{n} \\
&=
\mu
\end{align} 
\item Showing that the Sample variance is an unbiased estimator of the population variance:
\begin{align*}
\mathbb{E}[S^2] 
&=
\mathbb{E}\left[\frac{1}{n-1}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} (X_i-\bar{X})^2\right] \\
&=
\frac{1}{n-1}\mathbb{E}\left[\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}(X_i^2-2X_i\bar{X}+\bar{X}^2)\right] \\
&=
\frac{1}{n-1}\mathbb{E}\left[\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}X_i^2-n\bar{X}^2\right] \\
&= 
\frac{1}{n-1}\left[\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} \mathbb{E}[X_i^2]-n\mathbb{E}[\bar{X}^2]\right] \\
&=
\frac{1}{n-1}\left[\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} (\sigma^2+\mu^2)-n(\sigma^2/n+\mu^2)\right] \\
&=
\frac{1}{n-1}(n\sigma^2+n\mu^2-\sigma^2-n\mu^2)\\
&=
\sigma^2
\end{align} 

\end{enumerate}
